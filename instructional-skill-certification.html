Instructional Skills Certification


An internationally recognized Instructional Skills Certification program, the ISW assists instructors in transferring their knowledge and skills to others through the adoption of a teaching methodology that has proven success.

International Accreditation


AWENS facilitates ISW around the world.has expanded across the world, facilitating ISW in Cuba and Jamaica. Many centres of higher education have begun to adopt ISW for their staff as part of the onboarding process including McMaster University which has made ISW mandatory for their engineering faculty. AWENS has personally provided ISWs in Cuba and Jamaica to a great success. Completion of this workshop certifies the participant which is recognised at many institutions around the world.

The Process


The ISW is split over multiple days, totalling 24 hours of lesson time. This time can be organized to fit your organization's schedule. Over the duration of the workshop, each participant will give multiple short presentation using the teachings of the instructor.

Each presentation is filmed, allowing the participant to analyse in preparation for the next session. Additionally, peer comments are given to highlight areas of strength and improvement.

Why It Works


A study from Kwantlan Polytechnic University as well as joint research from Ryerson and Western University found that instructors who completed the workshop reported improved student engagement in lessons and greater focus on the var ying education needs of students. Implementing additional techniques to the traditional lecture that cater to more students with different learning types led to more students understanding the topic. The skills you learn in the ISW will acilitate the greater flow of feedback for both instructor and student.

A Canadian Creation


The Instructional Skills Workshop (ISW) was created in 1979 at the University of British Columbia to address the growing issue of instructors who are incredibly knowledgeable but lack the skills to teach the next generation effectively. The workshop is built around small groups of five or less to provide personal feedback and encourage sharing of every participant.
